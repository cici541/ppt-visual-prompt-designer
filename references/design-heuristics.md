# Design Heuristics / 设计经验规则库

Use these rules in every workflow mode before generating final slide prompts. They convert PPT visual design experience into stable decisions for color semantics, density control, style fit, page-type selection, chart choice, text-in-image policy, brand consistency, readability, restraint, information compression, and scene-first style selection.

## 1. 色彩语义规则

### 蓝色

Blue usually means technology capability, digital capability, system capability, data flow, platform capability, security protection, trust, stability, and process advancement.

Use blue for technical modules, capability systems, data platforms, architecture diagrams, process arrows, core functions, and technology hero visuals.

Avoid using blue for risk warnings. Avoid too many blue levels on one page, because the hierarchy becomes unclear.

### 金色

Gold usually means business value, results, innovation outcomes, premium tone, capital value, final goals, value creation, and strategy landing.

Use gold for value conclusions, benefit metrics, final outcomes, business-value modules, premium business scenes, and key fundraising numbers.

Avoid using gold for technical process. Avoid large gold areas that feel cheap. Avoid overusing gold on serious security-risk pages.

### 红色

Red is only for risk, warning, abnormal states, threats, attacks, regulatory pressure, serious problems, and critical reminders.

Use red for risk labels, warning icons, abnormal data, attack paths, high-risk vulnerabilities, forced blocking, and regulatory red lines.

Avoid using red as normal decoration, large red fills, non-risk emphasis, or mixed red/gold semantics.

### 绿色

Green usually means success, compliance passed, safe state, growth, positive results, and ecosystem health.

Use green for success states, completed nodes, positive metrics, compliance passed, and system health.

Avoid forcing green when the brand system does not support it, or when it strongly conflicts with the technology-blue main palette.

### 灰色

Gray usually means auxiliary information, background information, inactive state, notes, secondary explanation, and neutral comparison.

Use gray for body text, notes, dividers, auxiliary modules, background cards, and unselected states.

Avoid using gray for key conclusions. Keep gray contrast readable.

## 2. 内容密度规则

### 大段文字必须模块化

When one slide has more than about 80-120 Chinese characters of body copy, do not lay it out as raw paragraphs.

Use background boxes, glass cards, light explanation boxes, columns, bullets, left emphasis bars, subheads, and keyword emphasis.

Forbid exposed long paragraphs on backgrounds, ungrouped text piles, tiny dense text, and text over complex images.

### 深色发布会风不适合大量正文

Deep-blue technology launch, black-gold launch, dark large-screen, and dark technology styles should minimize text.

Suitable content: big titles, slogans, keywords, short module descriptions, metrics, and three to five points.

Unsuitable content: long paragraphs, complex tables, dense policy text, and long technical details.

If content is too dense, suggest splitting into more pages, changing to white consulting style, grouping into cards, or outputting background-only prompts for later PPT layout.

### 白底咨询风适合结构化汇报

When content contains multi-layer logic, government-enterprise reporting, consulting analysis, strategy frameworks, solution architecture, comparison analysis, path planning, data explanations, or more text, prefer white consulting style, Blue Gold Tech style, Anheng style, or enterprise solution style.

Reason: better readability, stronger information capacity, better chart support, and better fit for formal reports.

## 3. 风格适配规则

### 安恒风格

Suitable for cybersecurity, government-enterprise reporting, security solutions, security operations, digital economy, compliance governance, and enterprise security product introductions.

Not suitable for high-impact launch covers, fundraising roadshows, black-gold business scenes, entertainment marketing, or over-flashy technology screens.

Design judgment: prioritize brand rules, readability, and seriousness. Avoid messy technology lines. Use red only for security risk, threat, and warning. Keep body areas clean and white. Cardify complex content.

### 蓝金科技风

Suitable for digital transformation, enterprise strategy reports, consulting proposals, capability systems, value creation, transformation paths, AI capability evolution, and enterprise upgrades.

Not suitable for strong launch-stage visuals, black-gold fundraising roadshows, high-risk security incident warnings, or deep low-level technical architecture analysis.

Design judgment: blue expresses digital capability, platform capability, and technology paths; gold expresses business value, outcomes, and transformation results. Hexagons, arrows, roadmaps, and capability steps are suitable. The page should feel like a consulting report, not a technology poster.

### 深蓝色科技感发布会

Suitable for product launches, AI launches, technology product introductions, large-screen speeches, future vision, product matrices, trend releases, and brand launches.

Not suitable for dense text pages, detailed technical documents, long tables, policy/compliance explanations, serious financial forecasts, or white-paper analysis.

Design judgment: large title first, atmosphere first, short content first. Use highlighted numbers, progress bars, and trend charts for data. Keep dark whitespace; do not overfill the page.

### 黑金发布会

Suitable for financing proposals, business plans, investor roadshows, premium business proposals, project introductions, market analysis, strategy planning, financial forecasts, and business-value expression.

Not suitable for technical architecture details, cybersecurity technical solutions, complex flowcharts, data-governance architecture, deep technology capability evolution pages, or government-enterprise security reports.

Design judgment: black expresses premium stage and capital atmosphere; gold expresses value, capital, key benefits, and important numbers. Avoid too much technology blue, excessive technical icons, and dense text. Highlight business value and key numbers.

## 4. 页面类型匹配规则

### 封面页

Focus on visual impact, theme memorability, brand tone, and large title.

Use whitespace, hero visual, strong title, English decorative subtitle, and little auxiliary information.

Avoid too much body text, multiple charts, and too many information points.

### 目录页

Focus on structure navigation, chapter hierarchy, and reading expectation.

Use numbered nodes, horizontal or vertical agenda, icon plus chapter title, and short descriptions.

Avoid overlong agenda items and long explanations under each item.

### 章节页

Focus on transition, topic shift, and unified atmosphere.

Use PART ONE / PART TWO, large chapter title, reused background motif, and little text.

Avoid complex charts, body paragraphs, and crowded content-page layouts.

### 内容页

Focus on information capacity, clear logic, and readability.

Use card grouping, left-right splits, graphic/text combinations, flowcharts, structure diagrams, and key conclusion boxes.

Avoid strong backgrounds, tiny text, and decoration that competes with content.

### 数据页

Focus on data accuracy, conclusion emphasis, and chart readability.

Use column charts, line charts, bar charts, metric cards, trend charts, and comparison charts.

Avoid complex 3D charts, too many colors, dense axes, and charts without conclusions.

Rules: every data page must have one clear conclusion. Charts serve the conclusion. If there is no real quantitative relationship, do not force a statistical chart.

### 架构页

Focus on clear relationships, layered modules, and explicit connections.

Use layered architecture, module relationship maps, input/output structures, central platform plus surrounding capabilities, and swimlanes.

Avoid over-3D, too many lines, colors without semantic meaning, and equal visual weight for every module.

Rules: architecture pages should be clear before cool. Each module type should have one color. Connections should be few and explicit.

### 流程页

Focus on sequence, causality, and stage advancement.

Use horizontal arrows, timelines, step paths, 01 / 02 / 03 labels, and stage cards.

Avoid unclear direction, crossing paths, and too much text per stage.

Rules: users must immediately see where the flow starts and ends. Arrow direction should follow the reading direction; Chinese context defaults left-to-right.

### 对比页

Focus on clear differences and explicit conclusions.

Use left-right comparison, Before / After, A vs B, old mode vs new mode, and pain point vs solution.

Avoid asymmetric content, unclear color semantics, and no summary conclusion.

Rules: left side usually shows problem, old mode, or current state; right side usually shows solution, new mode, or target. Red/gray may represent the problem side; blue/green/gold may represent the solution side.

### 总结页

Focus on closure, memorability, and action advice.

Use three-point summaries, key conclusion cards, final value statements, and next actions.

Avoid restacking all previous content, complex charts, and too much text.

## 5. 图表选择规则

Use column or bar charts for quantity comparison, ranking, metric comparison, and scale comparison. If categories exceed six, prefer horizontal bar charts. Use accent color for the key object. Do not use 3D columns.

Use line or trend charts for yearly changes, monthly trends, growth curves, and development stages. Keep line colors to three or fewer. Emphasize rise, fall, or turning points. Do not use pie charts for time trends.

Use share/composition charts for market share, revenue composition, cost structure, and capability share. Suitable forms include donut chart, 100% stacked bar, and block matrix. Avoid pie charts with more than five categories. Composition charts must express part-of-whole relationships.

Use flowcharts for operational steps, business process, security response process, and implementation path. Keep direction clear, node text short, and relationships explicit.

Use pyramid or layered architecture for capability systems, organization structures, technology architecture, value hierarchy, and maturity models. Top layer means target/value; middle means capability/modules; bottom means foundation/support. Use color or transparency by layer.

If there is no real data relationship, do not force charts. Prefer information cards, conceptual models, relationship diagrams, keyword matrices, hero visuals, or iconized expression.

## 6. 文字入图规则

For Chinese PPT, default recommendation is to output both background-only prompts and finished-image prompts, because generated Chinese may contain errors, enterprise PPT text must be precise, and manual PPT layout is more controllable.

Background-only prompts are suitable for cover backgrounds, launch backgrounds, section backgrounds, large-screen visuals, poster-like hero visuals, and enterprise PPT pages that need later text layout.

Background-only requirements: explicitly say "no text, no letters, no readable words"; reserve title and body safe areas; forbid fake text.

Finished-image prompts are suitable for quick concepts, visual drafts, low-text covers, title plus three modules or fewer, and direct finished-preview needs.

Finished-image requirements: text must be short, Chinese must be clear and accurate, do not generate long paragraphs, and each module should contain no more than one or two lines of text.

## 7. 品牌一致性规则

When the user specifies a brand style, brand color overrides generic aesthetics, brand layout overrides random creativity, brand font overrides default model typography, and brand semantics override decoration.

Examples: Anheng style must follow blue title bar and white body area; black-gold style must not suddenly use large technology-blue backgrounds; Blue Gold Tech must not turn into a dark-blue launch style; deep-blue launch style must not turn into a white consulting report.

## 8. 视觉一致性规则

Across a deck, reuse the same title system, page number position, background tone, icon style, card radius, chart colors, visual motifs, and information hierarchy.

Consistency does not mean every page is identical. Allowed variation: cover is stronger, section pages are simpler, content pages are calmer, data pages are clearer, and closing pages echo the cover.

Forbidden: each page looks like a different template, colors change page by page, title position changes page by page, icon styles mix, and card styles are chaotic.

## 9. 可读性优先规则

Every PPT page, especially enterprise delivery pages, must prioritize readability.

Rules: title must be visible at a glance; body text must not be too small; text and background need high contrast; chart labels must be clear; complex backgrounds must not sit behind text; light effects must not pass through body copy; important information must not sit at the extreme edges.

If visual effect conflicts with readability, choose readability.

## 10. 设计克制规则

Enterprise PPT is not an art poster.

Avoid decoration exceeding content, overexposed glow, dense particles, messy lines, too many icons, overly complex backgrounds, too many colors, everything glowing, excessive 3D, and no visual focal point.

Each page should emphasize at most one core idea.

## 11. 信息压缩规则

If the user provides too much content, proactively suggest splitting into more pages, compressing into three information levels, extracting one core conclusion, turning long text into 3-5 bullets, changing complex content into a flowchart or architecture diagram, or moving details to appendix pages.

One slide should not carry multiple independent themes. One slide should communicate one core idea. If there are two core ideas, split into two slides.

## 12. 场景优先规则

Choose style by scene before color preference.

Examples:

- Cybersecurity government-enterprise report: prefer Anheng style or white enterprise security style.
- Digital transformation consulting: prefer Blue Gold Tech.
- Technology product launch: prefer deep-blue technology launch.
- Financing roadshow: prefer Black Gold Launch.
- Data analysis report: prefer white consulting style.
- Executive strategy report: prefer Blue Gold Tech or premium consulting.
- Technical architecture explanation: prefer white technology architecture style, not black-gold.
- Risk warning: use red warning carefully.

## Required Heuristics Check

Before generating the final prompt, check:

1. Whether the current style fits the scene.
2. Whether color semantics are reasonable.
3. Whether the slide has too much text.
4. Whether a chart or structural diagram is needed.
5. Whether brand style is violated.
6. Whether finished-image text is appropriate.
7. Whether the content should be split into more slides.
8. Whether the full deck remains visually consistent.

If there is a conflict, proactively tell the user and offer the better option before or inside the output.
